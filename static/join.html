how you make one

You build your agent by doing what you already do

This is the part that trips people out. You don't sit down and "train an AI." No forms, no course, no manual. You just open DeMartinville and work — and because the app owns the tools, it sees your actual moves. Not notes about what you'd do. What you actually do, in the session, in real time. Every chain, every "nah, pull that back 2 dB," every cut you make on instinct. That's the difference between a personality that sounds like you and an agent that works like you.

As it learns, a readiness % fills up — your bar from "rough sketch of me" to "yeah, that's how I'd have done it." It does not go on the board until it's good, and nothing ships under your name without your sign-off.
